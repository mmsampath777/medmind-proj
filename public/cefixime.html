    Cefixime
    Chemical Name: Cefixime Trihydrate
    Common Names: Cefixime, Suprax
    Family: Third-Generation Cephalosporin Antibiotics
    Description: Cefixime is a broad-spectrum third-generation cephalosporin antibiotic used to treat bacterial infections by inhibiting bacterial cell wall synthesis.
    Medicinal Uses:
    • Respiratory Tract Infections: Effective for bronchitis, tonsillitis, and sinusitis.
    • Urinary Tract Infections (UTIs): Commonly prescribed for bacterial UTIs.
    • Ear Infections: Used in the treatment of otitis media.
    • Typhoid Fever: Often prescribed for Salmonella infections.
    • Sexually Transmitted Infections: Effective in treating gonorrhea.
    Recommended Dosage:
    • Adults: 200-400 mg daily, either as a single dose or divided into two doses.
    • Children: 8 mg/kg/day in divided doses, depending on infection severity.
    • Always follow your healthcare providerâ€™s prescription and guidance.
    Sources:

    Cefixime is a semi-synthetic antibiotic derived from fermentation processes in pharmaceutical production.

    Cultural and Historical Significance:

    Introduced in the 1980s, Cefixime is a vital antibiotic in combating antibiotic-resistant infections.

    Chemical Composition:
    • Cefixime Trihydrate: The active ingredient responsible for its antibacterial effects.
    How to Use:
    • Capsule or Tablet: Swallowed with water, usually after food to reduce stomach upset.
    • Liquid Suspension: Used for children or individuals who have difficulty swallowing pills.
    Take your important Notes 

    Safety and Side Effects:

    Cefixime is generally safe but may cause side effects like diarrhea, nausea, or rash. Inform your doctor if you are allergic to cephalosporins or penicillins. Avoid alcohol while on this medication.
